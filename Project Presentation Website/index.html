Enhancing Image-Based Fashion Assistants with Conditioned Diffusion Models

Eyup MENEVSE, Mehmet Eren SOYKOK, Ahmet UMAN
SUPERVISOR: Prof. Dr. Nazli IKIZLER CINBIS

Abstract

Diffusion-based generative models have opened new possibilities for personalised fashion technologies. We present a multimodal system that synthesises realistic clothings directly on a user’s image, conditioned simultaneously on (i) a free-form natural-language description of the desired outfit, (ii) a user-defined spatial mask, and (iii) a semantic segmentation map of body parts. The proposed architecture couples a frozen Stable Diffusion backbone with a ControlNet branch that injects spatial information at every U-Net resolution, while CLIP-style text embeddings provide semantic guidance. Trained on scraped images and VITON-HD dataset augmented with GPT-4o fashion captions, our model achieves SSIM 0.843, PSNR 18.49 dB, LPIPS 0.073, FID 0.55 and KID 0.40±0.21—surpassing state-of-the-art virtual try-on methods.
